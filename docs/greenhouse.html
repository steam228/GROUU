Greenhouse IoT

Local automation for a greenhouse — air and soil sensing driving fans, a vent servo, grow lights and a pump, all over WiFi and MQTT. This was an early GROUU strand; it is archived and not in active development, kept here as reference for the reusable pieces it fed into.

▢  Archived.  This node isn't in active development. Records and files are being added soon — the material below is the design as it stood, and its reusable parts live on in the WiFi node and actuator module.
How it worked

Sense the room, drive the room

A single local controller reads the greenhouse climate and root zone, publishes it over MQTT, and switches the actuators that keep conditions in range. It stays on the local WiFi track — deliberately separate from the long-range LoRaWAN nodes — and its reference implementation is the GROUU WiFi node.

Intended local architecture (from the design notes)

[ DHT22 · LDR · soil-moisture ×2 ]
              │
           ESP32 controller  ──►  fan · vent servo · grow-LED ×2 · pump
              │
           Mosquitto  ──►  InfluxDB  ──►  Grafana

No greenhouse-specific firmware is committed; the sensing follows the WiFi node and the switching follows the actuator module. Pin assignments were never finalised — one of the loose ends the archive leaves open.
