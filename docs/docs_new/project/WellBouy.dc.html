WellBouy — well-water quality node

An open reference design for monitoring a water source over LoRaWAN — temperature, dissolved solids, turbidity and pH. This page documents how the node works and how to reproduce it from the published source.

How it works

Sense, encode, transmit, sleep

Analog probes are conditioned by dedicated modules and scaled to ≤3.3 V into a shared ADS1115 16-bit I²C ADC; temperature is read digitally over OneWire. Readings are packed as CayenneLPP and sent as a LoRaWAN uplink, after which the node deep-sleeps between cycles.

Hardware · sensor stack

Four probes, one shared front-end

All modules are off-the-shelf, so the design is reproducible without custom silicon. Per-sensor status reflects firmware-driver progress.

01 · Temperature✓ driver done
DS18B20 probe

Waterproof digital probe on OneWire, 4.7 kΩ pull-up, 3.3 V. Clean °C to serial and uplink.

02 · TDS◑ next
DFRobot Gravity TDS

Conditioner on the switched 5 V rail, output divided into the ADS. Dissolved solids in ppm.

03 · Turbidity○ planned
DFRobot SEN0189

Optical head, cabled; a 10k / 6.8k divider brings the 5 V output into ADC range → NTU.

04 · pH○ planned
Phidgets 1130 + BNC probe

GigaΩ analog conditioner (pH 7 ≈ 2.5 V), two-point calibration at pH 4 & 7.

Shared ADC ADS1115 16-bit I²C, addr 0x48, at 3.3 V — analog sensors on A0–A3, DS18B20 stays digital.

Hardware · power

Two rails, solar-fed

3.3 V

Always-on — MCU, radio, ADS1115 and the DS18B20.

5 V

Switched — analog conditioners powered ~1 s per cycle through a high-side load switch.

Bat

1S Li-ion 18650 (LiFePO4 for cold sites), solar charger with MPPT — panel sized after bring-up current measurements.

How to reproduce

Prove the radio, then each sensor

The firmware is organised so anyone can rebuild it incrementally — flash one stage, confirm it on serial and TTN, move on. Register your own device on TTN (EU868, LoRaWAN 1.0.x, OTAA), set the payload formatter to CayenneLPP, and keep your keys in an untracked secrets.h.
