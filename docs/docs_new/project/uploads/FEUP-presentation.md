## Designing *within*

- **Tomico et al. (2012):** designer as inhabitant and **primary user**
- Knowledge is **situated** — produced through sustained, lived engagement
- Not designing *for* others, nor only *with* them

Why it works as a **research mode**:

- Accountability — you live the failure
- Authentic edge cases, need-driven inquiry

> 1st-person *research method* = the lens on a 1st-person *design practice*

## Where it led — the question

- Open source design is theorised as **collective and distributed**
- So: is individually-situated **1st-person practice part of it — or outside it?**
- Hypothesis: the situated maker is a **node in the open ecosystem**, not a departure from it
- A register of participation **invisible to project-centric instruments** → *ecosystemic participation*

## The artifact

**Open-source well water sensor**

- Measures: TDS · turbidity · temperature · pH
- **XIAO ESP32-S3 + Wio-SX1262 (LoRaWAN)**
- → The Things Network → **RPi 4** pipeline
  (Mosquitto · Node-RED · InfluxDB · Grafana)
- Deployed at **my own well** — 1st-person by construction
